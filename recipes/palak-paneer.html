Palak Paneer

A vibrant and delicious dish with soft paneer cubes in a creamy spinach gravy. Perfect for a vegetarian meal!

Ingredients

  • 400g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 500g spinach, washed and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cloves
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 green chili, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ cup heavy cream (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Prepare the paneer: If using store-bought paneer, simply cut it into cubes. If making your own, follow a separate recipe for homemade paneer.
  2. Blanch the spinach: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes. Drain and plunge into cold water to stop the cooking. Once cool enough to handle, squeeze out excess water and blend until smooth.
  3. Make the gravy: Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, and cinnamon stick. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add onion and cook until softened. Add garlic, ginger, and green chili (if using), and cook for another minute.
  5. Stir in tomato, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the spices are fragrant.
  6. Add the blended spinach and stir well. Season with salt and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  7. Add paneer cubes and heat through for 2-3 minutes. Adjust seasoning if needed.
  8. Stir in heavy cream (optional) for a richer gravy. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan.
